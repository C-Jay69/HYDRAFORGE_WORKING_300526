2. The M&A Reference Criteria
All LLMs must be instructed to evaluate the contract strictly against the following M&A checklist points. If a point is missing, weak, or unfairly drafted, it must be flagged.

Definitions & Recitals: Check for vague definitions, especially "Material Adverse Effect/Change" (MAE/MAC).
Purchase Price & Consideration: Check for earnout ambiguities, purchase price adjustment mechanisms, and working capital definitions.
Representations & Warranties: Verify existence and strength of Seller Reps (Organization, Capitalization, Financials, Taxes, Material Contracts, IP, Data Privacy/Cybersecurity, Litigation) and Buyer Reps (Authority, Financing/Certain Funds). Check for inappropriate materiality qualifiers.
Covenants: Check for pre-closing ordinary course covenants, negative covenants (restrictions on seller actions before closing), and post-closing obligations.
Conditions to Closing: Verify regulatory approvals (antitrust), third-party consents, accuracy of reps, and no MAE/MAC clauses.
Indemnification: Analyze Escrow/Holdback amounts, survival periods, baskets (tipping vs. true deductible), and caps (including carve-outs for fraud).
Termination Provisions: Check for drop-dead dates, break-up fees, reverse break-up fees, and specific performance clauses.
Exclusivity/Non-Competition: Look for non-shop/go-shop clauses and post-closing non-competes (duration and scope).
Boilerplate: Governing law (Delaware preferred), dispute resolution, and non-reliance/exclusive remedy clauses.
RWI (Representations & Warranties Insurance): Check if RWI is mentioned, retention amounts, and underwriting exclusions.

Step C: LLM 3 (The Adjudicator) - Final Synthesis & Scoring

Input: The M&A Reference Criteria + LLM 1's JSON output + LLM 2's JSON critique.
System Prompt: "You are the Managing Partner of an M&A firm. You have a review from an associate and a critique from a senior partner. 0-49: Fatally flawed, highly one-sided, or missing critical protections. Critical risk. Do NOT proceed.
